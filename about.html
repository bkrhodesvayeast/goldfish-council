Our Fish

We specialize in fancy goldfish breeds with an emphasis on healthy body shape and finnage. Each fish is quarantined and monitored before being listed. See the Goldfish Videos page for current fish with video previews, and reach out if you'd like more details on a specific one.

The veiltail is prized for its dramatic, flowing double tail and tall dorsal fin, giving it a showier profile than most fancy goldfish. Its body is deep and rounded, similar to a ryukin but without the shoulder hump, and mature fish can reach eight to twelve inches. Because that tail is so long and delicate, veiltails aren't strong swimmers and do best in a calm tank alongside other slow-moving fancy goldfish rather than fast, nippy tankmates.

The breed traces back to Philadelphia in the 1890s, when a fringetail import was crossed with a short-tailed telescope-eye goldfish, eventually giving rise to the line known as the Philadelphia veiltail. Today's veiltails also include Chinese and English strains and can be found with either standard or telescope eyes.
